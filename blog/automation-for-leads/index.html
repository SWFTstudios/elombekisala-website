Automation for leads: capture, CRM, follow-up

February 10, 2025 · 5 min read

Once a lead hits your form, what happens? Manual entry is slow and error-prone. This post outlines a simple flow: form → CRM → email (or Slack) so you respond faster and keep data in one place.

Core flow

Form submission (Webflow, Typeform, etc.) triggers an automation. Step 1: create or update a contact in your CRM. Step 2: add to an email sequence or send a notification to your team.

Tools

Make.com and Zapier both work. Choose based on your CRM and email tool. Many CRMs have native integrations; use them when possible for reliability.

Best practices

Keep forms short. Map fields clearly (name, email, source). Test the flow before going live. Add a simple confirmation page or email so leads know they were received.
