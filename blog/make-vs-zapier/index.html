Make vs Zapier for marketing automation

February 25, 2025 · 5 min read

Both connect apps and automate workflows. Zapier is easier for simple “if this then that” chains. Make (Integromat) is more visual and often cheaper at scale; you build scenarios with modules and branches.

Zapier

Best for: quick setups, non-technical users, lots of app templates. Pricing scales by task count. Good when you have a few key zaps and don’t need complex logic.

Make

Best for: multi-step flows, conditional logic, and cost-effective ops. Scenarios can have many steps per run. Slightly steeper learning curve but more control.

Choosing

Start with Zapier if you want the fastest path. Switch or add Make when you hit limits or need more steps per automation.
